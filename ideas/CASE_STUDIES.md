Purpose:

This document defines the structure, writing style and required content for all case studies.

Every project featured on the website must follow this format.

---

# Case Study Principles

## Objective

A case study should answer:

1. What problem existed?
2. Why was it difficult?
3. What did Milind do?
4. How was it solved?
5. What was the result?

---

# Writing Style

Use:

* Clear language
* Real challenges
* Specific details
* Technical explanations
* Business outcomes

---

Avoid:

* Marketing fluff
* Buzzwords
* Generic claims
* Empty statements

---

# Overview

## Purpose

Explain the project in 2-3 paragraphs.

---

## Template

```txt id="4dlgrs"
<Project Name> is a <project type> designed to help <users> achieve <goal>.

My role involved <responsibilities> while working closely with <teams>.

The project required balancing performance, scalability and user experience while operating within the constraints of a large fintech platform.
```

# Challenge

## Purpose

Explain the problem.

---

## Questions

```txt id="uiv9zj"
What problem existed?

Why was it important?

What constraints existed?

What made it difficult?
```

---

## Example

```txt id="7rlj1m"
The platform needed to support a growing user base while maintaining fast page load times and a consistent user experience across devices.

Performance bottlenecks, legacy code and complex integrations created challenges that needed careful planning and execution.
```

# Golden Rule

The purpose of a case study is not to impress people with technology.

The purpose is to demonstrate how technical decisions solved real business problems and delivered measurable outcomes.

A recruiter should finish reading a case study and think:

```txt id="xkps57"
This person understands how to build products.
```
